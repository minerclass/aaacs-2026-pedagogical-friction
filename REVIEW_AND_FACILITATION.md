# AAACS 2026 Presentation Review and Facilitation Guide

## Review of the source deck

The 23-slide source deck had a strong scholarly spine and already addressed both reviewer concerns at a headline level. Its main limitation was not content depth; it was audience position. Most slides asked the audience to receive a finished argument rather than participate in the complicated conversation the argument calls for.

The interactive rebuild preserves the full-hour arc while changing the presentation rhythm:

- Claims alternate with audience judgments, case analysis, and design work.
- Reviewer 1's cognitive science / curriculum theory / Ong tension becomes an explicit intellectual hinge.
- Reviewer 2's request for stronger curriculum-studies pathways becomes an interactive pathway section rather than a single list of names.
- The productive/exclusionary distinction becomes a live case instead of only a conceptual guardrail.
- The conference theme of enclosure and subversion runs through classroom, platform, institutional, and political-economic levels.
- The persistent resource drawer prioritizes public proposal pathways and primary evidence sources rather than raw research folders or working documents.
- The Ong timeline, theory bridge, distributed-curriculum map, subversion practices, and public-scholarship routes are audience-controlled explorers.
- The praxis activity now builds a complete situated statement that participants can copy directly into conference chat.

## Reviewer-response language

### Reviewer 1

The paper does not use cognitive science as a sufficient theory of curriculum. Learning science helps explain why some forms of effort support durable understanding; curriculum studies asks who defines the purpose of that effort, how it shapes the subject, and how institutional and technological power structure the conditions of study. Ong functions as the hinge because his media ecology relocates cognition inside a historically changing symbolic environment.

### Reviewer 2

Pedagogical friction enters curriculum studies through more than application to classroom practice. It concerns currere and subject formation, Huebner's critique of technical rationality, the hidden curriculum embedded in frictionless platforms, and fugitive or subversive practices that preserve study against enclosure. The framework asks where curricular authority has moved and how educators and communities might interrupt that movement.

## Facilitation notes

- Ask participants to use chat for short responses so the online room remains active.
- On the Ong timeline, invite the room to choose which media transition most changes the meaning of curriculum.
- On the theory-bridge slide, let a participant choose which lens to foreground before revealing the synthesis.
- On the friction-lab case, resist giving a fast answer. The missing contextual information is the point.
- On the distributed-system map, ask the audience to choose the site where intervention currently feels most possible.
- On the curriculum-studies pathways, name fugitivity as a critique the framework must answer rather than a practice it claims.
- On the dimensions slide, distinguish the three learner-facing dimensions from infrastructural friction as their enabling or constraining institutional condition.
- Use the practice explorer as a menu rather than reading all six practices.
- Let participants assemble a praxis statement and use **Copy for chat** to share it.
- Treat criticism of the framework as evidence of a complicated conversation, not a threat to the presentation.
- Keep the research-design slides brief unless the audience asks for methods detail.
- Name the proposal-stage boundary plainly: the qualifying paper established the conceptual argument, the Chapters 1–3 proposal defines the inquiry, and dissertation findings do not yet exist.
- Describe the design as a Merriam-aligned interpretive case study with a qualitative-dominant convergent mixed methods design; do not use the older collective instrumental case-study label.
- Use `R` to open all public resources, `N` for speaker notes, and `O` for the slide overview.

## Privacy boundary

The deck links to public resources, proposal-orientation tools, and primary evidence sources. It does not collect or transmit audience responses. Do not add identifiable participant data, private district information, credentials, unpublished human-subjects material, or future study records to a public version.
